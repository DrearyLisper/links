## AI & Agents

- [Every minute you aren't running 69 agents, you are falling behind](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2026/03/11/running-69-agents.html) — geohot pushes back on AI anxiety and hype, arguing that AI is just another step in technological progress and that genuine value creation beats chasing trends. `ai` `agents` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-12)*

  **See also:**
  - [The Bitter Lesson](http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html) — Rich Sutton's essay arguing that general methods leveraging computation always win over human-knowledge-encoded approaches
  - [Situational Awareness](https://situational-awareness.ai/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/situationalawareness.pdf) — Leopold Aschenbrenner's long-form essay on the trajectory of AI development and what comes after current scaling

- [p(doom)](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2023/08/16/p-doom.html) — geohot argues that superintelligent AI development will be gradual and manageable rather than catastrophic, pushing back on Yudkowsky's doomer positions. `ai` `safety` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-12)*

  **See also:**
  - [AGI Ruin: A List of Lethalities](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/uMQ3cqWDPHhjtiesc/agi-ruin-a-list-of-lethalities) — Eliezer Yudkowsky's essay laying out why he believes misaligned AGI will kill everyone, the position geohot is responding to
  - [Why I Am Not As Concerned About AI As Some](https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=7784) — Scott Aaronson's measured take on AI risk, skeptical of the most extreme doom scenarios
  - [Intelligence Explosion Microeconomics](https://intelligence.org/files/IEM.pdf) — Eliezer Yudkowsky's technical paper on the dynamics of self-improving AI systems

## Philosophy & Culture

- [You Are a Good Person](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2025/09/02/you-are-a-good-person.html) — geohot argues that being a good person means producing more value than you consume, and calls for society to stop rewarding unproductive professions. `philosophy` `culture` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-12)*

  **See also:**
  - [How to Make Wealth](https://paulgraham.com/wealth.html) — Paul Graham's essay equating wealth with creating value people want, a complementary take on productive contribution
  - [On the Phenomenon of Bullshit Jobs](https://www.strike.coop/bullshit-jobs/) — David Graeber's essay arguing that a vast proportion of modern jobs create no real social value
  - [Meditations](https://standardebooks.org/ebooks/marcus-aurelius/meditations/george-long) — Marcus Aurelius on duty, virtue, and living a life of genuine contribution

## Programming Languages & Lisp

- [European Lisp Symposium](https://www.european-lisp-symposium.org/) — Premier academic conference covering all Lisp dialects (Common Lisp, Clojure, Racket, Scheme, Emacs Lisp, and more), with peer-reviewed papers, tutorials, and demos on language design, macros, and metaprogramming. `lisp` `conference` `pl` *(added: 2026-03-12)*

  **See also:**
  - [Beating the Averages](https://paulgraham.com/avg.html) — Paul Graham's essay on how Lisp gave his startup a competitive edge, a classic argument for expressive languages
  - [Practical Common Lisp](https://gigamonkeys.com/book/) — Free online book by Peter Seibel, one of the best introductions to Common Lisp
