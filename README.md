## AI & Agents

- [Every minute you aren't running 69 agents, you are falling behind](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2026/03/11/running-69-agents.html) — geohot pushes back on AI anxiety and hype, arguing that AI is just another step in technological progress and that genuine value creation beats chasing trends. `ai` `agents` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-12)*

  **See also:**
  - [The Bitter Lesson](http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html) — Rich Sutton's essay arguing that general methods leveraging computation always win over human-knowledge-encoded approaches
  - [Situational Awareness](https://situational-awareness.ai/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/situationalawareness.pdf) — Leopold Aschenbrenner's long-form essay on the trajectory of AI development and what comes after current scaling

- [p(doom)](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2023/08/16/p-doom.html) — geohot argues that superintelligent AI development will be gradual and manageable rather than catastrophic, pushing back on Yudkowsky's doomer positions. `ai` `safety` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-12)*

  **See also:**
  - [AGI Ruin: A List of Lethalities](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/uMQ3cqWDPHhjtiesc/agi-ruin-a-list-of-lethalities) — Eliezer Yudkowsky's essay laying out why he believes misaligned AGI will kill everyone, the position geohot is responding to
  - [Why I Am Not As Concerned About AI As Some](https://scottaaronson.blog/?p=7784) — Scott Aaronson's measured take on AI risk, skeptical of the most extreme doom scenarios
  - [Intelligence Explosion Microeconomics](https://intelligence.org/files/IEM.pdf) — Eliezer Yudkowsky's technical paper on the dynamics of self-improving AI systems

- [I Told You So](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2026/02/13/i-told-you-so.html) — geohot reflects on his 2019 singularity prediction and argues that current tech development prioritizes power and control over human wellbeing. `ai` `tech` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-13)*

  **See also:**
  - [The Singularity Is Near](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/291229/the-singularity-is-near-by-ray-kurzweil/) — Ray Kurzweil's foundational book predicting exponential technological convergence, the optimistic counterpoint
  - [Surveillance Capitalism](https://shoshanazuboff.com/book/about/) — Shoshana Zuboff's book on how tech giants exploit behavioral data to accumulate unprecedented institutional power
  - [The Techno-Optimist Manifesto](https://a16z.com/the-techno-optimist-manifesto/) — Marc Andreessen's maximalist case for technology and growth, a useful foil to geohot's concerns about who controls it

- [Get Out of Technology](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2025/09/13/get-out-of-technology.html) — geohot criticizes those who entered tech for money rather than genuine passion, arguing they degrade the field and should leave. `tech` `culture` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-13)*

  **See also:**
  - [Hackers and Painters](https://paulgraham.com/hp.html) — Paul Graham's essay on the kinship between hacking and other creative disciplines, a classic statement of intrinsic motivation in tech
  - [A Message to Garcia](https://courses.csail.mit.edu/6.803/pdf/hubbard1899.pdf) — Elbert Hubbard's 1899 essay on initiative and self-direction, a century-old take on the same divide between those who do and those who just collect a wage

## Philosophy & Culture

- [You Are a Good Person](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2025/09/02/you-are-a-good-person.html) — geohot argues that being a good person means producing more value than you consume, and calls for society to stop rewarding unproductive professions. `philosophy` `culture` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-12)*

  **See also:**
  - [How to Make Wealth](https://paulgraham.com/wealth.html) — Paul Graham's essay equating wealth with creating value people want, a complementary take on productive contribution
  - [On the Phenomenon of Bullshit Jobs](https://www.strike.coop/bullshit-jobs/) — David Graeber's essay arguing that a vast proportion of modern jobs create no real social value
  - [Meditations](https://standardebooks.org/ebooks/marcus-aurelius/meditations/george-long) — Marcus Aurelius on duty, virtue, and living a life of genuine contribution

- [Gambling is Bad](https://geohot.github.io/blog/jekyll/update/2025/10/24/gambling-is-bad.html) — geohot argues that gambling signals a society where people have stopped believing their efforts matter, and that a culture of luck-seeking destroys the motivation to create value. `philosophy` `culture` `opinion` *(added: 2026-03-13)*

  **See also:**
  - [Skin in the Game](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/537828/skin-in-the-game-by-nassim-nicholas-taleb/) — Nassim Taleb's book arguing that bearing the consequences of your own decisions is essential to ethics and competence
  - [The Lottery as a Tax on the Mathematically Illiterate](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/why-do-people-buy-lottery-tickets/) — Scientific American piece on why people systematically misjudge gambling odds and what it reveals about hope and despair

- [What to Do](https://paulgraham.com/do.html) — Paul Graham argues that beyond helping others, the highest human act is to "make good new things" — a short meditation on creation as the purpose of thinking. `philosophy` `essay` *(added: 2026-03-13)*

  **See also:**
  - [How to Do Great Work](https://paulgraham.com/greatwork.html) — Paul Graham's exhaustive companion essay on the practical mechanics of producing work that matters
  - [The Nature of Order](https://www.amazon.com/Phenomenon-Life-Nature-Order-Luminous/dp/0972652914) — Christopher Alexander's book arguing that making things well is a moral act tied to the aliveness of the world

## Writing & Craft

- [Good Writing](https://paulgraham.com/goodwriting.html) — Paul Graham argues that writing which *sounds* good is more likely to be *right* — because the process of polishing prose forces clearer thinking and makes errors visible. `writing` `essay` *(added: 2026-03-13)*

  **See also:**
  - [Politics and the English Language](https://www.orwellfoundation.com/the-orwell-foundation/orwell/essays-and-other-works/politics-and-the-english-language/) — George Orwell's canonical essay on how vague writing enables vague and dishonest thinking
  - [The Elements of Style](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/37134) — Strunk & White's slim classic on clarity and economy in prose, free on Project Gutenberg
  - [On Writing Well](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/on-writing-well-william-zinsser) — William Zinsser's book on nonfiction writing, with a particular focus on stripping out clutter
